What is a QR Code?

A QR (Quick Response) code is a type of two-dimensional matrix barcode invented in 1994. Unlike traditional barcodes that hold information horizontally, QR codes store data both horizontally and vertically, allowing them to contain significantly more information—up to 4,296 alphanumeric characters. They are designed to be easily read by digital devices, such as smartphone cameras.

Types of QR Codes

  • URL QR Codes: The most common type. Scanning it opens a specific web page directly in the user's browser, eliminating the need to type out long links.
  • Text QR Codes: Encodes plain text. Useful for sharing serial numbers, brief notes, or offline data that doesn't require an internet connection to view.
  • WiFi QR Codes: Contains network credentials (SSID and Password). When scanned by a smartphone, it automatically connects the device to the WiFi network—perfect for cafes, offices, or home guests.
  • vCard QR Codes: Acts as a digital business card. Scanning it prompts the user's phone to save the encoded contact details (name, phone, email, company) directly to their address book.

Privacy & Client-Side Processing

This QR Code Generator runs entirely in your web browser using JavaScript. When you enter your personal information, WiFi passwords, or URLs, the data is processed locally on your device to generate the QR code image. Your data is never sent to or stored on any server. This ensures maximum privacy and allows the tool to function perfectly even when you are fully offline after the page has loaded.

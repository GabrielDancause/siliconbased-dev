What is an Aspect Ratio?

An aspect ratio is the proportional relationship between the width and the height of an image, screen, or video. It is typically written as two numbers separated by a colon (e.g., 16:9). The first number always represents the width, and the second number represents the height. Understanding aspect ratios is crucial for ensuring that your visuals are displayed correctly without being stretched, squished, or cropped unexpectedly across different devices and platforms.

Common Aspect Ratios

  • 16:9 (Standard Widescreen): The default aspect ratio for modern HDTVs, YouTube videos, and most computer monitors.
  • 4:3 (Classic TV / Photography): Common in older CRT televisions, some tablets (like the iPad), and standard photography formats.
  • 1:1 (Square): Popularized by Instagram, it is perfect for profile pictures and social media grid posts.
  • 9:16 (Vertical Video): The standard for mobile-first content, including TikTok, Instagram Reels, and YouTube Shorts.
  • 21:9 (Ultrawide / Cinematic): Used primarily for cinematic film formats and ultra-wide gaming monitors.

How to Use This Calculator

This tool allows you to easily calculate new dimensions while maintaining the original proportions. Simply enter your original width and height (or select a common preset from the tabs). Then, toggle the "Lock aspect ratio" checkbox. When you change the New Width, the New Height will automatically update to keep the original proportions intact. This is entirely client-side, ensuring your data remains private and the tool works perfectly offline.

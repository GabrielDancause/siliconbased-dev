Frequently Asked Questions

Which credit cards have no foreign transaction fee?
Many travel rewards cards, including the Chase Sapphire Preferred, Capital One Venture series, and various airline or hotel co-branded cards, waive foreign transaction fees. Notably, all Capital One cards (including no-annual-fee options like Quicksilver) charge 0% foreign transaction fees.
Is the Chase Sapphire worth the annual fee for travel?
Yes, for frequent travelers. The Chase Sapphire Preferred has a $95 annual fee but offers no foreign transaction fees, premium travel insurance, and high reward rates on dining and travel. The sign-up bonus alone usually offsets the fee for several years.
Do Capital One cards charge foreign transaction fees?
No, Capital One does not charge foreign transaction fees on any of its US-issued credit cards. This applies to premium cards like Venture X as well as free cards like SavorOne and Platinum.
What is a foreign transaction fee?
A foreign transaction fee is a surcharge (usually 1% to 3%) billed by a credit card issuer on purchases made outside the United States or processed by a foreign bank. Using a card with 0% foreign transaction fees is highly recommended for international travel.
Which is the best credit card for international travel?
The best card depends on your spending habits. For premium perks, the Capital One Venture X or Amex Platinum are top choices. For a mid-tier all-rounder, the Chase Sapphire Preferred is excellent. For a no-annual-fee option, the Wells Fargo Autograph or Capital One SavorOne are strong contenders.

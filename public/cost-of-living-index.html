Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest city for digital nomads?
Based on our 2026 data, cities in Southeast Asia like Da Nang, Vietnam, and Chiang Mai, Thailand consistently rank as the most affordable, with estimated monthly budgets often below $1,400 USD including rent, food, and coworking spaces.
How much does it cost to live in Chiang Mai?
A typical digital nomad can expect to spend around $1,300 - $1,500 USD per month in Chiang Mai. This covers a modern city-center studio ($400-$500), frequent eating out at inexpensive restaurants ($2-$4 per meal), and a coworking space membership.
Is Lisbon expensive for digital nomads?
Lisbon has become significantly more expensive over the last few years. While historically cheap, a central studio now averages over $1,500 USD, pushing the total monthly nomad budget to around $2,700 - $3,000 USD, placing it among the pricier European destinations.
What is the best city for remote workers?
The "best" city depends heavily on your budget and climate preferences. Chiang Mai offers unbeatable value, Buenos Aires provides great culture at low costs, and European cities like Berlin or Barcelona offer excellent infrastructure but require a much higher budget.
How much money do you need to be a digital nomad?
To be comfortable globally, a baseline income of $1,500/month is required for affordable hubs (Southeast Asia, Latin America). For Western Europe or North America, you will need upwards of $3,000 - $4,500+ per month to maintain a comparable lifestyle.

Methodology

Our 2026 data is compiled from a variety of sources including Numbeo and crowd-sourced digital nomad communities. "Monthly Budget" is a custom calculation assuming a single person's living expenses (groceries, transport, utilities), a 1-bedroom apartment in the city center, and an estimated $150/mo allowance for coworking spaces. Values are represented in USD and are subject to currency fluctuations.

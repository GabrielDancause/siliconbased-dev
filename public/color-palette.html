Color Harmonies Explained

Creating a beautiful design starts with a solid color palette. Using proven color theory harmonies ensures your colors work well together naturally.

Complementary Palettes

A complementary color palette uses colors that are opposite each other on the color wheel. This creates the highest level of contrast, making the design elements pop. It's excellent for drawing attention to specific areas like buttons or calls to action.

Analogous Palettes

Analogous color palettes involve colors that are situated next to each other on the color wheel. They match well, creating serene and comfortable designs. Analogous palettes are often found in nature and are pleasing to the eye.

Triadic Palettes

A triadic color scheme utilizes colors that are evenly spaced around the color wheel. They offer high contrast while retaining harmony. Triadic palettes are vibrant, even if you use pale or unsaturated versions of your hues.

Tetradic (Double Complementary) Palettes

The tetradic (or double complementary) scheme is the richest of all the schemes because it uses four colors arranged into two complementary color pairs. This scheme is hard to harmonize and works best if you let one color be dominant while the others serve as accents.

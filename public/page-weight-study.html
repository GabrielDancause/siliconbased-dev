Key Findings

Media

Media sites are the heaviest

News and media publications consistently deliver the largest payloads, often weighed down by unoptimized images, videos, and heavy ad networks.

Tech

Tech companies lead the way in optimization

Technology websites are generally the most lightweight, employing better caching, minification, and modern delivery techniques.

Fat Web

The 5MB barrier is easily broken

A surprising number of top sites send more than 5MB of data to the client just to load the homepage.

Grades

Strict grading highlights the disparity

We grade A for under 500KB, B for 500KB-1MB, C for 1-2MB, D for 2-5MB, and F for over 5MB. Very few sites manage an A.

Methodology

We used a custom Node.js crawler to send an HTTP GET request to the homepage of 100 top websites across 7 distinct industries (Tech, Media, Ecommerce, Finance, Social, Travel, Gaming).

The "Page Weight" measured is strictly the size of the initial HTML response body in bytes. It does not account for secondary requests (like images, external CSS, or JS files) unless they are inlined into the initial HTML payload.

Grading scale: A (< 500KB), B (500KB - 1MB), C (1MB - 2MB), D (2MB - 5MB), F (> 5MB).

Percent Encoding & Decoding Guide

Every day, millions of URLs break because of simple spaces or special characters. A URL encoder translates these unprintable or reserved characters into a universally accepted format known as percent encoding.

Why Use a URL Encode Online Tool?

URLs are restricted to a specific subset of the ASCII character set. If you need to include a space, a foreign character, or a reserved symbol like & or ? within a query parameter, it must be percent-encoded. For example, a space becomes %20, and an ampersand becomes %26.

encodeURI vs encodeURIComponent Explained

When you URL decode or encode in JavaScript, you typically use one of two functions:

  • encodeURI(): Use this when you are encoding a complete URL. It leaves characters like :, /, ?, and & untouched, ensuring the URL structure remains valid.
  • encodeURIComponent(): Use this when encoding a specific component, such as a query parameter value or a path segment. It encodes almost everything, including / and &.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a URL encoder?

A URL encoder translates characters into a format universally accepted by web browsers and servers. It replaces reserved or unsafe characters with a '%' followed by two hexadecimal digits, ensuring the URL is transmitted correctly.

How to URL decode a string online?

What is percent encoding and why is it used?

Percent encoding, also known as URL encoding, is a mechanism for encoding information in a Uniform Resource Identifier (URI). It is used to safely transmit data containing spaces, special characters, or non-ASCII characters over the web by converting them into valid ASCII formats.

How to URL encode online securely?

You can URL encode online securely using our tool, which processes all data locally in your browser. No data is sent to external servers for encoding or decoding, ensuring your sensitive URLs and query parameters remain completely private.

What is the difference between encodeURI and encodeURIComponent?

encodeURI is meant for full URLs and ignores protocol and domain delimiters like '://', '/', '?', and '&'. encodeURIComponent is designed for specific query parameters or path segments and encodes everything, including those delimiters, to prevent them from breaking the URL structure.

What is a Text Diff Checker?

A text diff (difference) checker is a utility that compares two pieces of text and highlights the exact changes made between them. Whether you're comparing code, reviewing an edited document, or trying to find a tiny typo in a configuration file, a diff tool highlights exactly what was added, removed, or modified.

This tool performs a line-by-line comparison and further breaks down modified lines to show character-level differences, making it incredibly easy to spot subtle changes.

How does it work?

Our Diff Checker uses an advanced algorithm derived from the classic Longest Common Subsequence (LCS) approach. When you click "Compare", the tool:

  • Splits both the original and modified texts into individual lines.
  • Identifies lines that have been added (green) or removed (red).
  • For lines that share similarities but are not exact matches, it runs a secondary character-level comparison to highlight exactly which letters or words changed.

Features of this Tool

  • Side-by-Side View: Displays the original text on the left and the modified text on the right (stacked on mobile).
  • Character-Level Diffing: Highlights the exact words or characters that changed within a modified line, not just the whole line.
  • Ignore Options: Toggle options to ignore leading/trailing whitespace and case sensitivity to focus only on meaningful structural changes.
  • Client-Side Processing: All diff computations run entirely in your web browser. No data is sent to a server, ensuring complete privacy and offline functionality.

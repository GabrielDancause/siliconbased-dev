We crawled 100 of the world's most popular websites and analyzed their HTTP security headers. The results are worse than you'd expect.

Key Findings

82%

HSTS is the most adopted header

Strict-Transport-Security leads adoption, but even this basic header is missing on 18% of top sites.

52%

Only half use Content-Security-Policy

CSP is the most powerful defense against XSS — yet nearly half of top websites don't implement it at all.

19%

Permissions-Policy is widely ignored

Less than 1 in 5 sites control browser features like camera and microphone access. Most sites leave these APIs wide open.

2%

Cross-origin isolation barely exists

COEP, required for SharedArrayBuffer and high-resolution timers, is implemented by just 2% of sites.

Methodology

How we collected this data

Sample: 100+ of the most visited websites globally, spanning tech, media, e-commerce, finance, social, travel, and entertainment.

Method: HTTPS GET request to each domain's root URL. We recorded all HTTP response headers and checked for the presence of 10 standard security headers.

Grading: A = 7+ headers, B = 5-6, C = 3-4, D = 1-2, F = 0 headers present.

Limitations: We check header presence, not quality. A site could have a weak CSP (unsafe-inline) and still get credit. Some sites may serve different headers to different user agents or regions.

Reproducibility: Raw data is available. Crawl performed March 2026 from a US-based server.

Methodology

This study compiles the top marginal income tax rates, VAT (sales tax), and tax bracket counts for over 40 countries, updated for the 2026 tax year. Data is primarily aggregated dynamically from TradingEconomics for macroeconomic rates and PwC tax summaries for specific bracket structures. Where precise 2026 legislation is pending (such as the expiration of the TCJA in the US), estimates are based on the scheduled reversion to baseline laws. Missing data points are strictly maintained as unavailable rather than estimated to preserve accuracy.

Frequently Asked Questions

Which country has the highest income tax?

Based on our 2026 data, countries like Sweden, Denmark, and Austria frequently rank at the top with highest marginal income tax rates approaching or exceeding 50-55%.

What are the US federal tax brackets for 2026?

For 2026, barring new legislation extending the TCJA, the US federal tax brackets are scheduled to revert to their pre-2018 structures. The top marginal rate is expected to return to 39.6% from 37%.

Do any countries have 0% income tax?

Yes, several countries have a 0% personal income tax rate, including the United Arab Emirates (UAE) and Saudi Arabia, although they may levy other taxes like VAT or corporate taxes.

How does Canada's tax rate compare to the US?

Canada generally has higher top marginal tax rates compared to the US, often exceeding 50% when combining federal and provincial taxes, whereas the US top combined rate is typically lower, though this varies significantly by state/province.

What is the average tax rate in Europe?

The top marginal tax rate in Western Europe typically averages around 40-50%, often accompanied by substantial VAT rates (around 20%) and social security contributions.

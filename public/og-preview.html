What is Open Graph?

The Open Graph protocol was created by Facebook to allow any web page to have the same functionality as any other object on Facebook. Essentially, it allows you to control what snippet of information is displayed when someone shares a link to your website on social media platforms.

Why do social previews matter?

When people share your links, a good looking preview card dramatically increases click-through rates. By default, platforms will scrape your page and try to guess the best title, description, and image to show. Providing explicit Open Graph (og:) tags guarantees your content is presented exactly how you want it.

Common Open Graph Tags

  • og:title - The title of your page. Should be concise and compelling.
  • og:description - A brief description of the content, usually 1-2 sentences.
  • og:image - The URL of the image to represent the content. Most platforms recommend 1200x630 pixels.
  • og:url - The canonical URL of your page.

While Twitter has its own set of twitter: tags, it falls back to Open Graph tags if specific Twitter tags are missing. LinkedIn and Discord also rely heavily on Open Graph data.
